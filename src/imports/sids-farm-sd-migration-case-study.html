1Background

What was the challenge?

Sid's Farm — a premium dairy brand based in Hyderabad — operates a large-scale direct distribution network with field sales executives managing 1,000+ retail stores across multiple routes. Their existing SFA (Sales Force Automation) platform, Incitesol, was running at a store-limit-based pricing model that had grown expensive relative to the value delivered. Key capabilities like route optimisation, target-vs-achievement dashboards, digital signatures for POD, and asset management were absent. The project involved auditing the full operational process, documenting it as an SOW, evaluating Bizom as a replacement, and leading the migration while maintaining operational continuity.

2Operational Process

The T+1 order-to-delivery cycle

The entire S&D operation runs on a T+1 model — orders are placed the day before delivery (T−1), invoiced and dispatched on the delivery date (T), and payments reconciled the following day (T+1). This was documented as a formal SOW covering 5 modules and 15+ operational steps.

01
One-time
Store Onboarding
Field exec creates store via app with GPS, route, warehouse. S&D assigns margin template and credit limit. Store activates for ordering.
02
T−1 (Day Before)
Order Placement
Sales team or retailer places order for next-day delivery. Orders stay in Draft until cutoff, then auto-confirm. Amendments go through approval workflow.
03
T (Delivery Day)
Invoicing & Fulfillment
Stock logged in at warehouse. Invoices generated for confirmed orders (E-invoice for GST-registered). Routes assigned with optimised store sequences. Stock-out logged per driver.
04
T (On Ground)
Delivery & Returns
Driver delivers to each store, logs POD and GRN. PRNs and replacements recorded in real time. Post-delivery, returns cross-validated at warehouse and inventory reconciled.
05
T+1 (Next Day)
Payment & Reconciliation
Cash and online payments logged, verified, and approved. Each payment credited to store ledger against invoice debits. Group payments supported for 7/15-day cycle stores.
Ledger Model

Immutable, double-entry ledger tracking

  • →Order placements logged as debits against customer account
  • →Cash/online payments logged as credits post-approval
  • →PRNs treated as credits against order value
  • →Replacements = inventory movements, no financial impact
  • →Every entry time-stamped, user-tagged, and immutable
Approval Workflows

Role-separated approval gates

  • →Order amendments require approval before moving to invoicing
  • →Cash collections require approval before ledger reconciliation
  • →Online payments verified before credits are applied
  • →PRNs cross-validated at warehouse post-delivery before inventory update
  • →Store credit limit relaxation requires dedicated approval
3Incitesol App — Documented Flows

What was being used

Before migrating, the full Incitesol field app was documented screen by screen — creating a reference for requirements mapping, gap analysis, and Bizom configuration. The app covered 10+ functional modules used daily by field executives.

Screen 01

Login & Role-Based Access

Username/mobile login provisioned via Incitesol Admin Portal. Role-based access ensured only authorised field executives could log in and access their assigned stores and routes.

Screen 02

Home Screen — Daily Control Centre

Geo Check-In/Check-Out recorded start and end of workday. Displayed total stores assigned (1,040+), total routes (22), customer visits, complaints, and total journey distance in km.

Screen 03

Store List & Route View

Full list of assigned stores filterable by route and area. Primary gateway for all store-level activity — order placement, payment collection, and visit check-in. Each store showed due amounts and credit utilisation.

Screen 03.1

Store Actions Menu

Per-store action hub: Order Edit (invoice + PRN + Replacement tabs), Payment (invoice selection and collection), Visit Check-In (geo-tagged), Navigation Map, and Change Address with GPS coordinate capture.

Screen 03.1.1

Order Placement Interface

SKU-level order entry by packet size and quantity with live credit limit and due amount visibility. Supports Invoice, PRN, and Replacement in three tabs. Shows past orders and pending indent drafts.

Screen 03.1.2

PRN & Replacement Records

Historical view of all Purchase Return Notes — item, reason, and quantity. Replacement section logs non-billable product swaps for stock correction and traceability at each store.

Activity Modules

Add Store / Lead Management

New store onboarding with name, GST, email, address, and route assignment. Prospects entered as Leads first, then converted to active stores before orders can be placed.

Activity Modules

Stock In / Stock Out

Warehouse-level stock movement tracking. Stock-In captures product arrival from plant/warehouse. Stock-Out logs dispatch per driver route. Includes reconciliation completion tracking (84% completion shown in example).

Activity Modules

Attendance, Leave & Track

Geo-tagged check-in/check-out with auto-calculation of total working hours. Daily travel route logged in Track module. Leave application with date, leave type, and remarks submitted directly from the app.

4Migration Decision

Why the switch to Bizom

The migration decision was driven by a structured three-way evaluation (Incitesol, Bizom, Raastha.ai) across price, feature coverage, and operational fit for a growing dairy distribution network. Bizom emerged as the strongest choice on both economics and capability.

Before · Incitesol

What was missing

  • No route optimisation for delivery sequencing
  • No target vs. achievement dashboards
  • No KRA / performance dashboards for managers
  • No target setting at user or outlet level
  • No focus SKU tagging for priority products
  • No digital signature capture (POD/GRN)
  • No asset management module
  • No competition stock survey capability
  • No configurable survey / activity forms
  • No notification management (web → mobile)
  • Pricing: Store-limit based — costly at scale
  • Monthly cost: ~₹1,30,000
After · Bizom

What gets unlocked

  • Optimised delivery routes with store sequencing
  • Target vs. achievement dashboards (SKU/brand/category/beat)
  • KRA and user performance dashboards for reportees
  • Target settings at user and outlet level
  • Focus SKU tagging for priority products
  • Digital signature capture from mobile
  • Full asset management (add, remove, verify)
  • Competition stock survey at outlets
  • Configurable surveys (text, dropdown, image capture)
  • Notification management (web → mobile push)
  • Pricing: Per-user based — scales efficiently
  • Monthly cost: ~₹25,000 (negotiated rate)

7New Capabilities Gained

Beyond cost savings, the migration brings 9 capabilities that were entirely absent in Incitesol — directly addressing operational gaps in the field sales, delivery, and performance management workflows.

🗺️

Optimised Route Planning

Bizom generates optimised delivery sequences for each driver's route — reducing fuel costs, improving on-time delivery rates, and giving warehouse teams a predictable dispatch schedule.

Delivery Management
🎯

Target vs. Achievement Dashboards

Real-time visibility into sales performance against targets — broken down by SKU, brand, category, beat, and distributor. Enables territory managers to course-correct during the month rather than at month-end.

Sales Analytics
📊

KRA / Performance Dashboards

Manager-level dashboards showing performance of each reportee — attendance, order value, store coverage, and target achievement — replacing manual Excel-based tracking.

Performance Management
✍️

Digital Signature Capture

POD (Proof of Delivery) and GRN (Goods Receipt Note) can now be digitally signed on mobile — eliminating paper-based processes and providing a verifiable, timestamped delivery confirmation trail.

Delivery Compliance
🏷️

Priority SKUs can be tagged and surfaced at the top of the order interface — ensuring field executives prioritise high-margin or launch products during store visits without relying on memory or verbal briefings.

📦

8Outcomes

Key outcomes & contributions

This project touched strategy, operations, product evaluation, and documentation simultaneously — delivering both immediate financial savings and a platform foundation for Sid's Farm's distribution growth over the next 3–5 years.

01

₹1,05,000/month (80.8%) reduction in SaaS licence costs

Identified a direct ₹1.26L annual saving by migrating from Incitesol's store-limit-based model to Bizom's per-user pricing at a negotiated rate of ₹25,000/month for 50 users — making the migration self-funding within the first year even after accounting for the one-time ₹4,00,000 setup cost.

02

Full SOW documentation of a 5-module T+1 distribution process

Authored a comprehensive Statement of Work covering Store Onboarding, Order Placement, Invoicing & Fulfillment, Delivery & Returns, and Payment Reconciliation — with step-level detail on approval workflows, ledger logic, and system expectations. This became the reference document for Bizom configuration and ongoing team training.

03

Structured 3-platform competitive analysis across 70+ feature dimensions

Conducted a granular feature comparison across Incitesol, Bizom, and a third vendor — mapping 70+ capabilities across Order Management, Delivery, Inventory, Payments, Executive Operations, Invoicing, and Analytics. The analysis provided a defensible, data-backed case for the migration decision to leadership.

04

Closed 9 critical feature gaps that were absent in Incitesol

The migration delivered 9 capabilities that were entirely absent in the previous platform — including route optimisation, target-vs-achievement dashboards, digital POD/GRN signatures, focus SKU tagging, asset management, competition surveys, and configurable activity forms — transforming the field app from a basic order-taking tool into a full-stack SFA platform.

05

Incitesol app fully documented for requirements transfer and onboarding continuity

Created a screen-by-screen documentation of the Incitesol field app — covering all 9 functional modules with flow descriptions and screenshots — ensuring no operational knowledge was lost during migration, and giving the Bizom configuration team a clear reference for feature parity requirements.

06

Pricing proposal negotiated to a below-list rate with included training

The final Bizom proposal achieved a 53% reduction from Bizom's standard list price (₹1,300/user → ₹800/user) with a discounted one-time setup cost (₹8,50,000 → ₹4,00,000) and 15 days of master data support and training included — demonstrating commercial negotiation alongside technical evaluation.

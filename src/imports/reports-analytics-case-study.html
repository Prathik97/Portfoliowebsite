1Background

What is the Reports Module?

TFW's reporting system is the primary business intelligence layer of the platform — giving facility managers, business group operators, and account-level administrators visibility into every financial, operational, and tenancy event happening across their facilities. The system spans 200+ individual reports across 20 categories, each with its own requirement specification covering column definitions, filter logic, sort options, footer aggregations, permission levels, and delivery methods. Reports operate at three hierarchy levels — account, business group, and facility — and were migrated from a legacy v10 system with full data continuity.

4Reporting Framework

How the reporting system works

Behind the 200+ reports is a shared infrastructure layer — governing how reports are generated, accessed, delivered, filtered, and migrated. Every report follows the same specification structure, ensuring consistency across the entire catalogue.

🏗️

Three Access Levels

Reports are scoped to Account (full business), Business Group (multi-facility clusters), or Facility (single location) — with the Super Summary and high-level financial reports restricted to management-level permissions at the BG/Account tier.

🔐

Permission-Gated Access

Each report category has its own access permission — financial reports require different permissions than operational ones. Management reports (like the Super Summary) have a separate UI access layer beyond the standard report workspace.

🎛️

Configurable Filters

Every report specifies its available filters — Date Range, From/To Days, Sort By, Group By, or specific identifiers like Order Number. Filters are report-specific and wired to the underlying data model for each category.

📐

Standardised Spec Format

Every report was documented in a requirement format covering: general description, access level, use case, generation frequency, filters, heading fields, column definitions (with business logic notes), footer aggregations, and a linked sample PDF.

🖨️

Delivery & Generation

Reports are accessible via the Report Workspace (WC) and can be generated as PDFs for print or email delivery. Certain reports like the Purchase Order support reprint via a specific filter (order number), while others like the Super Summary are generated on-demand for a specific effective date.

🔄

v10 → v11 Migration

All reports were designed with migration in mind — each requirement included migration notes ensuring legacy data (rate histories, adjustment records, access codes, contact logs) was carried forward correctly and reflected accurately in v11 report outputs.

📊

Footer Aggregations

Every report specifies exactly what aggregations appear in the footer — total counts, sum totals, averages, percentages, and subtotals grouped by category or unit type. These aggregations are business-logic-specific, not simple column sums.

🏢

Corporate Reports

A dedicated Corporate Reports workspace exists alongside the standard Report WC — providing account-level and multi-facility consolidated views used by business owners and regional managers rather than individual facility staff.

📂

Saved Document Migration

The migration work included a Saved Document Migration effort — ensuring all previously generated reports and tenant documents from v10 were preserved and remained accessible in v11's document storage, linked to the correct tenants and facilities.

6Outcomes

Key outcomes & contributions

Building a 200+ report system required a disciplined approach to specification, categorisation, business logic documentation, and migration — far beyond just generating data tables.

01

200+ reports specified, categorised, and built across 20 domains

Designed and delivered a complete reporting catalogue covering every operational and financial dimension of a self-storage business — from access code audits and rent adjustment status to income reconciliation and management-level occupancy dashboards — with every report individually specified using a standard requirement format.

02

Multi-level access architecture — account, business group, and facility scoping

Implemented a three-tier report access model where reports are scoped to the correct organisational level — high-level financial and occupancy reports like the Super Summary are restricted to management-level BG/account access, while operational reports (overlocks, access codes, move-in activity) are accessible at facility level by standard staff.

03

Super Summary — a 9-column, 8-section management dashboard in report form

Designed and built the Super Summary as the most complex report in the system — combining financial totals, payment receipts, unit occupancy metrics, economic occupancy calculation, aging buckets, delinquency event breakdowns, and prepaid summaries across nine time-period columns including rolling prior months and a same-month year-over-year comparison.

04

Business-logic-accurate column definitions across all 200+ reports

Every column in every report was documented with its precise business logic — including edge cases like Retained Rent (rent kept when not prorating out at move-out), Variance Above/Below (rate deviation from standard), Economic Occupancy (effective rate vs gross potential), and NSF/Prepaid handling — ensuring reports reflected the actual financial state rather than simplified data dumps.

05

Full v10 → v11 migration coverage with data continuity

Every report specification included migration notes — ensuring legacy data from the v10 platform (rate histories, adjustment records, contact logs, access codes, saved documents) was correctly carried forward and reflected accurately in all v11 report outputs from day one of the platform migration.

06

Standardised specification format enabling scalable report development

Established a repeatable requirement document format — general description, access level, use case, frequency, filters, heading, column definitions, footer aggregations, sample PDF link — that was applied consistently across all 200+ reports, making it possible to onboard new report requirements quickly and hand them off to engineers without ambiguity.

01 · Background

What is the Move Out Module?

Move Out is the formal tenant offboarding workflow in TFW — the moment a tenant vacates a unit and the financial relationship is settled. The module handles billing resolution (proration, charge deletion, or outstanding balance carry-forward), deposit and refund management across five payment pathways, post-move-out tenant actions (deactivation, do-not-rent alerts), and generation of all exit documentation — all within a structured 3-step flow accessible from six entry points in the platform.

02 · Workflow

The 3-step Move Out flow

Every move-out follows a structured sequence — from billing decisions and deposit handling, through a charge summary, to post-move-out balance resolution and documentation delivery.

01
Step One

Tenant, Unit & Billing Details

  • Confirm tenant, ledger, unit, and move-out date
  • Review late notification penalty (waivable)
  • Choose resolve charges method — Do Nothing, Delete Unpaid AR, or Prorate Move Out
  • View list of all unpaid AR line items
  • Set prorate amount if prorating (user-editable)
  • Add per-unit comments and reason for vacating
  • View unit and tenant deposit details for refund calculation
02
Step Two

Summary

  • Full breakdown of all charges to be debited or credited
  • Deposit return amounts displayed separately
  • Net balance clearly shown — positive (balance due) or negative (credit due)
  • Final confirmation step before committing move-out
03
Step Three

Post Move Out

  • Balance resolution — choose how to handle due or credit balance
  • Payment split showing deposits vs. other charges
  • Activate "Do Not Rent" alert on the tenant
  • Deactivate the tenant (if eligible — no active units, zero balance)
  • Generate and deliver all configured documents
  • Move-out document, thank-you letter, invoice, ledger history, contact log, demographics
03 · Balance Resolution

Handling every financial outcome

After move-out, the ledger will have either a balance due (tenant owes money) or a credit due (tenant is owed a refund). The module provides distinct resolution paths for each scenario — configurable as facility defaults and overridable per move-out.

Balance Due · Tenant Owes

Positive Balance Options

When the tenant has an outstanding amount after move-out, staff choose one of three resolution paths:

Apply Payment Default
Hold balance on ledger (ledger cannot be deactivated while balance exists)
Write off balance — reduces balance to zero, posted to a configured charge category (e.g. Uncollected Rent)
Credit Due · Refund Owed

Negative Balance Options

When the tenant has overpaid or deposits are being returned, staff choose one of five refund paths:

Apply Cash Refund Default
Hold refund on ledger (ledger cannot be deactivated)
Assess to tenant_refund charge category
Apply to bank via ACH transfer
Apply to credit card — reverse to original card
04 · Resolve Charges

Handling outstanding bills at exit

At step 1 of move-out, the user decides how to handle any outstanding or over-paid rent charges — choosing one of three strategies that directly affects the final ledger before balance resolution.

🚫

Do Nothing

Outstanding charges remain on the ledger as-is. The tenant is expected to settle all unpaid bills. No adjustments are applied to the billing record. Best for facilities that handle collections separately.

Default
🗑️

Delete Unpaid AR

Staff choose to delete all or selected unpaid accounts-receivable entries — writing off late fees or other outstanding charges that the facility decides not to pursue. All unpaid AR is listed for review before deletion.

Write Off
📐

Prorate Move Out

When the tenant has pre-paid future billing periods, the excess amount is calculated and refunded. Staff can see the number of periods and days already paid, and can manually adjust the prorate amount. Triggered by a configurable days-elapsed threshold at the facility level.

06 · Document Generation

Exit documentation

Six document types can be generated and delivered at move-out time — each configurable as a default in Move Out Configuration, with auto-populated tenant and unit data at time of generation.

01

Move Out Document

The primary move-out record — showing unit details, move-in/out dates, paid-to date, and a full recent transaction history with billed, paid, and balance columns. Auto-populated per tenant and unit.

02

Thank You Letter

A farewell letter sent to the tenant thanking them for their tenancy and inviting future business. Template-driven with facility name, address, phone, and manager details auto-populated.

03

Invoice

A final invoice generated at move-out capturing all outstanding or settled charges. Useful for facilities that issue formal invoices to tenants as part of their close-out process.

04

Ledger History

The complete transaction history for the tenant's ledger — every bill, payment, and credit — provided as a handover record. Included by default in move-out documentation.

05

Contact Log

A full chronological log of all communications with the tenant — emails, texts, in-person contacts, and system-generated events — delivered as part of the move-out record. Included by default.

06

Demographics

A summary of the tenant's demographic information captured during their tenancy — useful for facilities that need to maintain an offboarding record or carry forward contact details.

07 · Role Permissions

Access control model

Move Out actions are protected by four granular permissions — giving facility operators control over who can perform a move-out, edit its date, waive penalties, or override charge resolution defaults.

perform_move_out
Users can move tenants out of a unit. This is the base permission required to access the move-out workflow at all.
edit_move_out_date
Users can edit the effective move-out date. Without this permission, the move-out date defaults to today and cannot be changed.
waive_notification_penalty
Users can waive the late notification penalty amount during move-out when a tenant hasn't provided the configured advance notice period.
override_resolve_charges
Users can override the facility's configured default for Resolve Charges — choosing a different strategy (Do Nothing, Delete Unpaid AR, or Prorate Move Out) on a per-move-out basis.
08 · Outcomes

Key outcomes & contributions

Move Out sits at the intersection of financial accuracy, compliance, and tenant relationship management — requiring precision across billing logic, refund pathways, and configurable defaults.

⚖️

Flexible balance resolution across 8 distinct financial outcomes

Designed and built a dual-path balance resolution system — 3 options for a balance-due scenario and 5 options for a credit scenario — with facility-configurable defaults and per-move-out overrides, covering every common financial outcome at tenant exit.

📐

Configurable proration and charge write-off at exit

Built the Prorate Move Out logic to calculate excess rent paid by the tenant across future periods, with an optional days-elapsed threshold so proration only activates after a configurable cut-off — preventing unnecessary refunds for early-month exits.

💳

Multi-path refund delivery including ACH and credit card

Implemented five distinct refund pathways — cash, ledger hold, tenant_refund category, ACH bank transfer, and credit card reversal — so facilities can match their refund process to the tenant's original payment method and their own financial workflows.

📋

Complete exit documentation package

Built auto-populated generation of six document types at move-out — the move-out report (with full transaction history), thank-you letter, invoice, ledger history, contact log, and demographics — all configurable as facility defaults with template-driven data population.

🔒

Granular permission model for sensitive move-out actions

Designed a four-permission access control model separating the ability to perform a move-out, edit its date, waive penalties, and override charge resolution — giving facility operators fine-grained control over who can do what without restricting the base workflow.

👤

Post-move-out tenant lifecycle management

Built the post-move-out actions panel — enabling staff to activate a "Do Not Rent" alert and conditionally deactivate the tenant (gated on zero balance and no remaining active units) — ensuring tenant records are correctly transitioned in the system after exit.

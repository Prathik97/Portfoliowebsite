01 · Background

What is the Retail Sale Module?

Every self-storage facility sells merchandise to tenants — padlocks, boxes, packing tape, bubble wrap. The Retail Sale Module in TFW is a fully integrated commerce system that handles the complete merchandise lifecycle: suppliers are tracked as Vendors, items are catalogued in Inventory with three price tiers and barcode support, sold via a Point of Sale system that works for both tenants and walk-in customers, and returned through a flexible multi-search Returns flow with four refund pathways. A configuration layer and eight granular permissions give each facility control over exactly how buying, selling, and auditing works at their location.

02 · Two Core Pillars

Inventory Management & Point of Sale

The module is built on two interdependent systems — an Inventory Management layer that governs what exists and at what price, and a Point of Sale layer that handles the moment of transaction.

01

Inventory Management

A complete product catalogue supporting manual entry and barcode scanning for product ID capture. Each item carries three price tiers (Retail, Preferred/discounted, Wholesale for profit tracking), a linked vendor, a charge category, and reorder automation — including a configurable reorder point and preferred reorder quantity. Inventory Audit allows quantity correction with optional negative inventory support for edge cases where physical stock exists but isn't reflected in the system.

02

Point of Sale (POS)

A transaction screen supporting both registered tenants and walk-in customers. Items are added to cart manually or via barcode scanner, with default quantity of 1 and the option to use preferred (discounted) pricing. The system handles zero-quantity edge cases, negative inventory selling, and on-the-fly product addition. Payment is processed through all facility-configured payment methods. Post-sale, a configurable purchase order is generated and printed.

03 · Inventory Management

Product catalogue, reorder & audit

Inventory Management is the source of truth for all sellable products — capturing physical, financial, and logistical data per item, with controlled edit permissions and a dedicated audit workflow for quantity correction.

🔍

Barcode Scanner Support

Product ID and UPC can be captured via barcode scanner — automatically populating fields at both the Inventory creation screen and the POS cart. Manual entry is also supported as a fallback.

💲

Three-Tier Pricing

Each product carries Retail Price (standard sell price), Preferred Price (configurable discount), and Wholesale Price (purchase cost used for profit calculation). Preferred price can be set as default at POS via configuration.

🔄

Reorder Automation

Each product has a Reorder Point (quantity threshold that triggers a restock alert) and a Preferred Reorder Quantity (pre-filled default when restocking). The restock screen lists all items below their reorder point with editable quantities.

📋

Inventory Audit

A dedicated quantity-correction flow separate from standard editing. Supports both additive adjustments (add/remove X units from current stock) and absolute updates (set total to Y). Negative inventory supported with config flag and mandatory comment enforcement.

🏪

Kiosk Visibility

Each inventory item has a "Show at Kiosk" toggle controlling whether it appears on the tenant-facing kiosk or self-service portal. Allows facilities to restrict which products are available for self-checkout vs. staff-assisted purchase.

⚙️

Permission-Gated Editing

Product ID is locked from editing post-creation. All other fields (description, vendor, prices, charge category, reorder values, status) require the edit_inventory permission. Available Quantity is read-only — changeable only via the Inventory Audit access point.

04 · Point of Sale

The transaction experience

The POS system is the front-facing transaction layer — designed to serve both facility tenants and walk-in customers, with barcode scanner integration, preferred pricing, real-time stock awareness, and a configurable purchase order output.

01

Customer Selection

  • Link sale to an existing tenant by name or ledger
  • Process as a Walk-In Sale for non-tenant customers
  • Tax-exempt walk-ins require tenant record creation to capture tax ID
  • Walk-In Review screen tracks all walk-in transactions
02

Build the Cart

  • Add items by product ID / description (manual) or barcode scan
  • Barcode auto-populates product ID and sets quantity to 1
  • Change quantity per item as needed
  • Apply preferred (discounted) price on a per-item basis
  • Remove items from cart at any time
03

Stock Edge Cases

  • Zero-quantity item: prompt to add to inventory or sell into negative stock
  • Negative inventory selling allowed only if config flag is enabled
  • Log User ID, Product ID, and date for all negative-inventory sales
  • New products not yet in system: add to inventory on the fly and add to cart
04

Payment & Receipt

  • Total shown; payment accepted via all facility-configured methods
  • Sales tax applied per charge category configuration
  • Purchase order generated post-sale
  • Configurable number of copies printed automatically
  • Walk-In Review captures all transaction details for audit

Walk-In Sale Tenant

When the customer is not a registered tenant, all sales are tracked through a system Walk-In Sale tenant record — preserving transaction history for returns, refunds, and reporting without requiring a full tenant account to be created for every anonymous purchase.

Preferred Price Logic

The facility can configure POS to always default to the discounted preferred price — in which case the POS shows the item at the preferred rate and displays the total savings to the user. This can also be applied selectively per line item regardless of the global configuration.

Negative Inventory Tracking

When a sale pushes inventory below zero, the system logs User ID, Product ID, and sale date — creating an audit trail for each negative-inventory transaction. This supports stock reconciliation without blocking sales of physically available products.

Walk-In Review Screen

A dedicated screen surfaces all walk-in sale transactions with Transaction Number, Created Date, Purchase Order ID, Product Description, Amount Charged, Amount Paid, and Date — with Reverse and Delete actions, plus the ability to add manual charges or take payments directly from this view.

05 · Returns & Refunds

Multi-path returns with full refund logic

The Returns system lets facility staff process item returns initiated by tenants moving in or out — or by walk-in customers. Three search methods locate the original purchase, with quantity validation, refund-amount locking, and four distinct refund pathways.

Search Method 01

By Purchase Order

  • Enter Purchase Order ID to retrieve all items in that order
  • Can also scan the receipt ID to pull up matching items
  • All items shown by default; user selects which to return
  • Cannot process returns across two different purchase orders in one transaction
Search Method 02

By Purchase Date

  • Select a date to list all purchase orders from that day
  • Latest purchase order surfaced at the top and pre-selected
  • User can then select specific items across the day's orders
  • Useful for same-day returns without knowing the order number
Search Method 03

By Customer Name

  • Search by tenant name or walk-in customer record
  • Displays all historical purchases for that customer
  • Latest purchase shown at top and pre-selected by default
  • User selects items to return from the full purchase history
Item Selection Rules
  • →Items sorted alphabetically ascending within an order
  • →Selecting an item defaults quantity to 1
  • →Min quantity = 0, max = original purchased quantity
  • →+ / – buttons disabled at quantity limits
  • →Over-limit entry triggers error message
  • →Return amount locked — matches original billed price
  • →Inventory updated automatically on successful return
Refund Pathways
  • →Refund to Tenant Balance — credit added to tenant ledger
  • →Return & Refund (Cash) — cash returned to customer
  • →Return & Refund (Credit Card) — reversal to original card
  • →Return & Refund (Check/MO) — check or money order issued
  • →Refund to balance hidden if facility disallows prepay AND tenant has no unit
  • →On success: form clears, success snackbar shown
  • →On failure: form preserved, error snackbar shown
Edge Case Handling

Deactivated Tenant

If the associated tenant is inactive, the system provides an in-context "Activate" action — directly reactivating the tenant and ledger without losing return flow context. The user isn't navigated away to View Tenant.

Deactivated Ledger

Same activation pattern as deactivated tenant — the user is prompted to activate the specific ledger. Once reactivated, the return is processed against the same ledger. Cross-ledger calculations are deferred to future scope.

Deactivated Vendor

Returns for items from a deactivated vendor are blocked — the return option is disabled for those line items. Facilitates clean vendor offboarding while preventing unexpected financial entries against inactive suppliers.

Deactivated Inventory Item

Similarly, returns for items where the inventory record has been deactivated are disabled. Historical purchase records are preserved for audit purposes, but the active return flow is locked.

06 · Vendor Management & Deactivation

Supplier records & lifecycle decisions

Vendors are the upstream supply layer — each inventory item is linked to a vendor record, creating a dependency chain that must be carefully managed when a vendor relationship ends. The Vendor Deactivation feature required deliberate product decisions about how to handle in-flight inventory and outstanding returns.

Deactivation Design Decisions
Q: When should vendor deactivation be allowed?
When no items remain tagged to that vendor, or the facility is no longer sourcing from them. No new items can be created or linked to a deactivated vendor. Existing items can still be audited but not refilled via restock.
Q: What about items still physically in the facility?
Deactivating a vendor with existing inventory requires the user to first clear or reclassify those items. The user is informed to remove items or mark them as no longer in use before deactivation proceeds.
Q: Can returns be processed for deactivated vendor items?
Returns for items linked to a deactivated vendor are disabled in the Returns flow. The facility handles any exceptional cases outside the software — preserving data integrity without blocking the deactivation.
Q: Chain impact: Vendor → Inventory → POS → Returns
Vendor status cascades downstream: deactivated vendors block new inventory creation, prevent restock, disable returns for their items, and surface inventory items as "no more in use" — giving a clear operational handoff status without data loss.
07 · Configuration Layer

How facilities customise behaviour

Five configuration controls give each facility precise control over POS pricing defaults, inventory integrity rules, and purchase order output — without requiring any code changes across the platform.

Default
Default to Preferred Price
When enabled, the POS populates the price column with the discounted Preferred Price by default and shows the customer their total savings. Requires Preferred Price to be filled on each product (enforced at inventory level when this config is true).

Allow Negative Inventory
Permits inventory to fall below zero — both at POS (selling when stock shows 0) and during Inventory Audit (manually entering negative quantities). When disabled, neither POS nor Audit can push quantities below zero.

08 · Permission Model

Eight granular access controls

Retail Sale uses a granular permission model — separating concerns so that a staff member authorised to sell can do so without being able to modify the underlying inventory catalogue, and an auditor can adjust stock without being able to process sales.

sell_inventory

Users can sell items through the POS. The base permission for all transaction-taking staff. Without this, the POS screen is inaccessible.

negative_inventory

Users can sell items when stock shows zero or below — only visible and usable when the "Allow Negative Inventory" facility configuration is enabled.

add_new_inventory

Users can add a new inventory item directly from the POS if the product isn't yet in the system. Deliberately independent of edit_inventory — selling staff shouldn't be blocked by catalogue gaps.

edit_inventory

Users can add and modify products in Inventory Management — descriptions, vendors, prices, charge categories, reorder values, and status. The primary inventory catalogue management permission.

restock

Users can restock items through the Inventory Management restock workflow — adding quantities to products that have fallen below their reorder point.

return_inventory

Users can process item returns through the Returns flow — initiating refunds to ledger, cash, card, or check from previous purchase orders.

inventory_audit

Users can edit the available quantity of inventory items through the dedicated Inventory Audit screen — both additive adjustments and absolute quantity updates.

negative_inventory_audit

Users can set inventory quantities to negative values during Inventory Audit. Only visible when "Allow Negative Inventory" config is true. Separate from the POS negative-sale permission.

09 · Outcomes

Key outcomes & contributions

The Retail Sale Module required building a complete mini-commerce system inside a facility management platform — touching financial logic, stock management, supplier lifecycle, UX edge cases, and permission architecture simultaneously.

01

Complete 4-layer commerce system embedded in a non-commerce platform

Designed and built an end-to-end retail system — Vendor → Inventory → POS → Returns — that integrates seamlessly with the existing tenant ledger, payment method configuration, charge category system, and reporting infrastructure, without requiring a standalone retail application.

02

Three-tier pricing with configurable preferred price defaulting

Implemented a per-product pricing model with Retail, Preferred (discounted), and Wholesale tiers — wired to a facility configuration that can set the POS to always default to the preferred price with live savings display, enabling facilities to run loyalty or tenant discount schemes without manual intervention per sale.

03

Negative inventory system with full audit trail and granular permission separation

Built a negative inventory system that handles both POS sales and Inventory Audit quantity edits — with a configuration gate, a separate audit-specific permission, mandatory comment enforcement, and per-transaction logging (User ID, Product ID, date) — creating a complete audit trail for stock discrepancies.

04

Multi-path return system with four refund pathways and edge case handling

Designed a returns UX supporting three search methods (Purchase Order, Date, Customer Name), strict quantity validation with bounded min/max and disabled buttons at limits, a locked return amount matching original billing, and four refund pathways — including the conditional "refund to balance" that respects the facility's prepayment configuration.

05

Vendor deactivation cascade with downstream dependency design

Worked through a complex multi-question vendor deactivation design — defining how deactivation cascades from Vendor → Inventory → POS → Returns, deciding what happens to existing items when a vendor is deactivated, and deliberately blocking returns for deactivated-vendor items rather than silently failing — ensuring clean data integrity across the full dependency chain.

06

Eight independently scoped permissions preventing over-restriction and under-control

Designed a permission model where selling, inventory editing, auditing, restocking, and negative-quantity access are fully independent — so facilities can authorise POS-only staff to add new products at point-of-sale without granting full inventory management rights, and can separate negative POS selling from negative audit access.
